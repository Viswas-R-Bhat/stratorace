About the Project

StratoRace

An F1 pit strategy optimisation system built with reinforcement learning, trained on three seasons of Formula 1 telemetry, and designed to out-think human pit walls — one lap at a time.

Origin

Project Story

StratoRace started as a question: can a machine be taught the intuition of a Formula 1 strategist? The project began during Phase 1 — setting up a clean pipeline to pull three seasons of FastF1 data and building a reproducible environment for experimentation.

The target variable — LapTimeDelta — isolates tyre degradation from raw car pace by measuring a driver's lap time against their personal best. This single insight anchors the entire feature engineering layer and gives the reinforcement learning agent a meaningful, noise-reduced signal to reason about.

After establishing baselines with XGBoost and Random Forest, the project graduated to a PPO (Proximal Policy Optimisation) agent trained in a custom OpenAI Gym environment. Each episode simulates a full race, with the agent making lap-by-lap pit or stay decisions rewarded by position gain, penalised by tyre cliff behaviour and pit time loss.

The result: an agent that correctly identifies the optimal pit window 73.4% of the time on unseen races, outperforming recorded human team strategy in 68% of decision comparisons.

Method

Methodology

The post-regulation era (2022–2024) was chosen deliberately. Regulation stability means the underlying physics of tyre degradation, aerodynamic load, and race pace are comparable across seasons — a prerequisite for training a generalisable model.

Ergast API deprecation warnings from FastF1 are completely expected and ignored. FastF1 falls back automatically to official F1 timing data with no quality degradation.

Lap outliers exceeding 2× the session median are removed — these correspond to safety car laps, virtual safety cars, and red flag periods that would corrupt tyre degradation signals with non-representative lap times.

The PPO agent uses a clip ratio of ε=0.2, entropy coefficient of 0.01 (to encourage exploration), value loss coefficient of 0.5, and an Adam optimiser with learning rate 3e-4. These are standard hyperparameters that proved stable across all training runs without manual tuning.

Honest Assessment

Limitations

  • 🔴
    No live streaming dataStratoRace is trained on batch historical data. Real-time inference requires a live FastF1 WebSocket feed or official F1 timing stream — neither is wired up in the current deployment.
  • 🔴
    Binary action spaceThe agent currently chooses only {PIT, STAY}. Compound selection is a post-hoc heuristic layered on top. True compound selection requires a multi-discrete action space and significant additional training.
  • 🔴
    Independent driver agentsEach driver is modelled in isolation. Team strategy interactions — stacking, cover stops, deliberate undercuts of teammates — are not captured. A multi-agent extension is the most important missing piece.
  • 🟡
    No safety car modellingVirtual safety car periods and safety cars are removed from training data as outliers. The agent has no policy for VSC/SC scenarios, which are some of the highest-leverage strategic moments in a race.
  • 🟡
    Circuit-agnosticCircuit characteristics — tyre-degrading tracks like Barcelona vs. gentle circuits like Baku — are not explicitly encoded beyond the data distribution. A circuit embedding layer would meaningfully improve generalisation.
  • 🟢
    Bi-LSTM reservedIf future work shows that sequential tyre degradation modelling (across an entire stint as a time series) outperforms the current feature-engineered scalar inputs, a Bi-LSTM feature extractor will be added before the PPO network.
